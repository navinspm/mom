  • Post by Rajesh
Our “Go Green” initiative is all about doing our part for the global warming. The project was spear headed by R. Aadhityan, who also wrote this blog.  We chose an auspicious occasion i.e., a wedding between
G.Dinesh raja and M.Reshma 25-6-18, at Raya mahal kumbakonam and gifted “teak saplings” to guests who attended the ceremony. 

Here’s an account of Aadhityan’s take on the project :
 Trees for life, at the pace of  modernisation a phrase that reverberates  around every corner of  world. Who is out there not knowing about trees for rain,  global warming, green house effect, cabon offsetting .?certainly none. They do know what trees do to combat the adverse effects of industrialisation, as well.
Perhaps, here I should not be elaborating about the benefits of planting trees,  as that might sound very cliche to you all, instead I would like to share how I went about executing the project per se.
My dad Dr. Ramasamy is a founding member of MoM foundation and therefore  I was given the opportunity to lead this project.  On getting this chance,  the very first question I had to face was, why such a normal and not new project without any creative spirit..?  While there are many government and non government organisations carrying out this go green project at a higher level, what am i going to do here with my meager effort.? Will my effort go meaningless..? Yet i moved ahead, where in course of project i got pleasing answers for my all questions. 
Also the necessity of the outcome of project  was strong enough to win over all my want of thrill in a project. Finally I made up my mind to start up this so called ordinary one, but with an extraordinary pay off. 
Giving out complimentary  saplings to all the guests attending the wedding - here in poped up another  big question about the success of project . What surity is there for the saplings to get planted.? If at all they got planted what about thier nurturing.?  Here, we referred to an earlier same project that we did in my aunt's  marriage.  The guests's feed back was encouraging , ofcourse though not all saplings but few hundreds of them are trees now, a standing evidence that kicked out one more hurdle.
The last but huge stumbling block  was about me, am I that efficient  to lead a project..?  I  was just out from school, having no experience with this type of project, I was so nervous to accept this. But everything went well . Still I can remember the smiling faces of guests on seeing the saplings, some looking for a better sapling,  some fetched up few more saplings, others praising our effort on greener earth, those were really pleasing moments. A heart felt thanks to members of MoM foundation, their continous  support and encouragement  strenghtened me to push through this project. Its my privilege and pride to be a part of MoM foundation. 
  
Now I believe, some of these few hundred saplings  will grow up as trees and live for ages. They will stand stark and say loudly to mankind " No worries my men, we are here to care you ever.. a little time spent on us will be paid back as your endless good future ..".
